MF Doom

Daniel Dumile is an American hip-hop artist born British who went by serveral names like

  • Metal Fingers
  • King Geedorah
  • Viktor Vaughn
  • Zev Love X
  • DOOM

but mostly known as MF DOOM. He is known for his word play and his creation of a comic book story with him as the main villian. Fantastic Four and their fights with Dr.Doom is what heavily inspired his creations.

His Start

In 1988 Doom created a group called KMD with his younger brother known as DJ Subroc and another MC named Onyx the Birthstone Kid. He was calling himself Zev Love X during this time. A rep at A&R learned about KMD and got them signed to Elektra Records. They released their first album with Elektra in 1991 named "Mr.Hood". It became a minor hit through its singles "Peachfuzz" and "Who Me?".
Unfortunetly in 1993 memeber Subroc(Dooms younger brother) was hit by a car and killed before the second album was even released. This led the group to being dropped by Elektra Records within the SAME WEEK. From the loss of his brother Doom completely let go of his rap career from 1994-1997. Living in a difficult situtation, being homeless and sleeping on the benches of Manhattan. This led him to moving to Atlanta and swearing revenge to the music indusrty that deformed him terribly.



Birth of DOOM

In 1997, Doom began to freestyle secretly at open-mic events at a cafe in Manhattan. He would perform on stage without reveling his identity by wearing a sock over his head. He later started wearing a mask that was similar to Doctor Doom who is shown on the cover of is 1999 album Operation: Doomsday. He is to be seen always with the mask on even in photos and performances. He released three singles:

  1. Dead Bent (1997)
  2. Greenbacks(1997)
  3. The M.I.C(1998)
In 2001 he went by the name Metal Fingers and released an album that were just beats. Many of the beats that he created were later used throughout his work. In 2003, going by King Geedorah he released the album Take me to Your Leader. King Geedorah was credited as the producer and had featured guests on the songs. One of the features include himself as MF DOOM. The album Vaudeville Villian was released as well. It was under the name Viktor Vaughn, which is a play on Dr.Doom (his real name was Viktor Vaughn Doom).

His Recognition

In 2004 MF DOOM released the album MM..Food under the label Rhymesayers Entertainment. Then releasing the album Madvilliany with the producer Madlib the album became a success. The album recieved tons of publicity and was on the Rolling Stone, Washington Post, New York Times, and The New Yorker. This led to the first tour of Madvilliany. He later decided to take a bigger step to the mainstream in 2005 creating the group Danger Doom and releasing The Mouse adn the Mask. He collaborated with DJ Danger Mouse and released the album on October 11, 2005. He also collaborated with Cartoon Network's Adult Swim and featured characters and voice actors. This album would reach #41 on the Billboard 200.


MF DOOM continued to produce tracks in 2006 but the tracks were for Ghost face Killah for 2 albums. He also hosted the Adult Swim Christmas special and was a feature in the song November Has Come by the Gorillaz in their 2005 album Demon Days. Ghostface Killah and Doom worked on an album called Swift & Changeable rumored to be releasing soon but in fact did not. He continued to work on Adult Swim doing voice overs and ads. Late 2009, it was confirmed that Doom would release an album named MF and his name would change to just DOOM. The first single for the next Madvilliany ablum was dropped through Adult Swim in 2010 called Expektoriation. Unfortunetly Madvilliany 2 would not be released and later in 2020 MF DOOM would be pronounced dead. I dont really want to go in depth as how he passed but it was due to many mishaps in the hospital he was in and was neglected. It was a difficult time due to COVID, hospitals barely had room and prioritized patients with COVID.
